The White Oak (Quercus alba) is a majestic tree native to North America, 10 species are native to Canada. It holds a special place in Canadian history being one of the most important commercial hardwoods. The wood is waterproof, durable and elastic, it is used in lumber production, shipbuilding, railroad ties, barrels and firewood. Its distinctive lobed leaves turn a rich burgundy colour in the fall, adding to the country's scenic beauty. In the Kelowna region, white Oak trees contribute to the region's urban biodiversity, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species. Fun fact: White Oak wood is greatly valued for its robustness and durability, making it the preferred material for barrels employed in the maturation of high-quality wines and spirits. These barrels play a vital role in contributing to the growth of the Okanagan wine economy and culture.
